EV Batteries

Electric Vehicles run on lithium-ion batteries. This involves the creation of dedicated large-scale factories known as gigafactories, producing batteries by the thousands, and themselves contributing a large amount to global emissions, essentially taking the place of the emissions of a traditional car over its lifetime and transferring it to the beginning of the process.

According to environmental science researcher Florian Knobloch, battery production is the primary driver behind the increase in emissions in the manufacture of EVs.

These gigafactories are also often still powered by fossil fuels through direct industrial lines regardless of the sources of their host nation’s power grid due to the large amount of energy required, though strides are being taken to reduce the carbon footprint of these factories.

Raw materials such as cobalt and lithium, which are critical to the manufacture of lithium-ion batteries, are also energy intensive to mine and transport, further increasing the carbon footprint of EV batteries.

EVs are not a magical solution to the climate crisis, as simply replacing all 1.2 billion gas-powered cars with EVs will not reduce emissions due to the volume of batteries that will need to be produced, regardless of how clean the energy grid is.

According to Knobloch, society will have to change to fully reap the benefits that the decarbonisation of the energy grid and of transport will bring. Electric Vehicles are just one step in that direction. Embracing public transport as well as remote work and learning should be a key factor in reducing the harmful effects of climate change.
