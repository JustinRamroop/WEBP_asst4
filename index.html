What is this all about?

Over the course of the 2010s, electric vehicles (EVs) have become a massive industry, increasingly becoming visible on the worldâ€™s motorways.

Image credit: Matt Weissinger

During the 2010s and early 2020s, the EV industry went from selling tens of thousands of units per year to ballooning into a 300 billion dollar industry, with expected growth to shatter the trillion dollar ceiling by 2028, according to projections from Fortune Business Insights.

Image credit: Evocharge.com

Of note is the carbon footprint of EVs compared to the current status quo of vehicles which run on internal combustion engines (ICEs). Experts broadly agree that EVs are better for the environment than ICEs, which contribute to emissions at a far greater rate.

Image credit: Tesla

From flimsy promises and buzzy stocks to widespread adoption by the biggest car manufacturers, with broad support from governments around the world, electric vehicles are here to stay. Consumers are increasingly shopping for greener cars and the dropping cost of EVs are no doubt going to entice consumers.

Source: Getty Images

However, there is a dark side which is not discussed often in the public but presents a massive environmental and moral quandary for manufacturers of EVs to solve. EVs are not going away, nor should they, as they have been proven to be the way forward for green vehicular transportation.

But with the planet hanging in the balance, what shall the industry profit to gain the world, but lose its soul?
